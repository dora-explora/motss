This is actually Jill Rose, of Quartet/Women's Press (London), using
Simon's user code to follow up:

At our last sales conference, the Women's Press sales reps came to the
conclusion that mention of gay sexuality at the start of an ad/blurb
is a disincentive to some buyers, but mention half-way is ok, because
the prejudiced reader is interested by then, and the open-minded reader
was never a problem. The gay reader will seek out books concerning
gay sexuality (we hope).  This attitude may seem mercenary, but wider sales
allow us to publish more books for lesbians.

These opinions are my own, and not necessarily those of my employer
or any other person.
