I saw Querelle, and it is truly a movie to avoid unless you fit into
one of these catagories:  a Fassbinder freak, who will see anything
with his name on it, a "gay film" maven, who needs to see everything
on the subject, or a "bad film" connoisseur who truly relishes seeing
high intentions turned into low humor.

Genet's images are entirely literary--their beauty arises out of
the prisoner/poet transforming his ugly surroundings through the
artifice of writing.  Transferred to the screen, Genet's baroque
fantasies become overblown and ridiculous.  Fassbinder took an
entirely too literal approach to the book, instead of trying to
create a cinematic metaphor for Genet's feverish style. 

Although the subject matter might be called "mature", it is not
especially brutal or graphic by today's standards.  There are better
reasons to avoid this movie.

/Steve Dyer
