Here's some interesting reading from, THE GOOD NEWS IS THE BAD NEWS IS WRONG,
by Ben Wattenberg, '84, p.296-7.

He's talking about the supposed change in moral values the right is moaning
about.  He doesn't see it.

"Consider Homosexuality.  Lots of publicity, but apparently no change in behavior.  
There has been much talk about high school sex-education classes that discuss
homosexuality as an 'alternate life-style.'  We have seen much on television
news about homosexual marriage.  Conservatives have gone into orbit about the
legitimization and glorification of homosexuality in the popular culture:
music, movies, the dance, television, and so on.

But the latest studies indicate that, depending on the definitions used, about
5-10% of the adult population are practicing homosexuals.  That is roughly the
same incidence reported by Kinsey in the early 1950s.

Nor has there been an attitudinal change.  While all the homosexual hoopla
was going on, the National Opinion Research Center was in the field, asking
whether 'Sexual relations between two adults of the same sex . . . is always
wrong, almost always wrong, wrong only sometimes or not wrong at all.'  In
the 1973 poll 80% of the population chose wrong/almost always wrong.  In
1982, the percentage was 78%, well within the margin of sampling error.

As with other data concerning sexual attitudes, there are no solid time lines
available for earlier eras.  But negative opinion at around 80% is high
indeed, and could not have come down too much in any event."

His conclusion!  "Continuity.  Core values and traditional behavior withstood
a media wave."
